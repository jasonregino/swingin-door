Award-Winning Fish & Chips

Golden, crisp, and wrapped in newspaper, just as it should be.

Regulars will tell you the fish and chips are the reason they first walked through the door, and most of them never really left. Beer-battered, fried to order, and served in newsprint with a wedge of lemon.

Add half-pound burgers, fresh seafood, and a kitchen that stays open late, and you have a proper British pub that has fed San Mateo since 1955. The bar pours till two, and there is nearly always something going on.

A San Mateo landmark

Pouring pints since 1955

Over the years this bar has seen a bit of everything. Bing Crosby would quietly sip a beer at the end of the bar, Ronald Reagan once bought the room a round, and a young Kingston Trio played the upstairs dance floor in 1957. Today Warren Chapman keeps the British tradition going, right down to the fish and chips wrapped in newspaper.
