Award-Winning Fish & Chips

Golden, crisp, and wrapped in newspaper, just as it should be.

A San Mateo landmark

History of the Door

The Swingin' Door has been a hangout for noteworthy folks in San Mateo since 1955. A bit of the story.

Built in the 1940s, the Kehoe building was converted into a bar in December 1955 by the Fey Brothers, whose grandfather invented the slot machine. It was named The Swinging Door for the two swinging doors at the entrance. A 12oz mug of beer cost 20 cents.

Early 1956 saw pinup girl Betty Grable and bandleader Harry James frequent the bar while their horses ran at Bay Meadows. The upstairs dance floor went in that year to host a dance school for several years.

A young, struggling group called The Kingston Trio played upstairs in 1957. In 1958 someone lobbed a tear gas bomb into the bar room, and famous jockey Willie Shoemaker dropped in during the racing season. A seat belt was even bolted to a pillar for regulars who wanted to "buckle up" before driving.

Hugh Courtney hung the first bristle dartboard in 1963, and the first All-Star match between Bay Area and Los Angeles dart teams was played here in 1964. A go-go dancer did her thing that year while patrons talked about Carol Doda, The Beatles, and Vietnam.

Later in 1964, actor Ronald Reagan bought the bar a round while campaigning, and a longtime regular treated newly elected Congressman Pete McCloskey to a drink. In 1966, Len Titus planted the Bird of Paradise "tree" when it stood 18 inches tall. It is now the tallest in Northern California.

Through many owners and decades it always stayed a proper beer bar, with familiar faces on both sides of the taps and a dartboard that was never quiet. Around 1969 an irate person fired a gunshot through the front door into the end of the bar, thankfully closed at the time.

Shut for several months, the bar reopened in 1971 as The Prince of Wales, with the British decor it still wears today. From 1970 to 1973, crooner Bing Crosby would quietly sip a beer at the kitchen end of the bar, back when the Windsor Burger and the Fish & Chips were first gaining local fame.

In November 2006, Warren Chapman bought the British pub and brought back the original name, The Swingin' Door. Born in Manchester, England, Warren comes from a deep line of British servicemen. His grandfather, William Chapman of the 70th Battery RFA, earned the Battle of Mons medal, his great uncle PTE John Chapman rode with the 11th Hussars, and his father Raymond Chapman was a Grenadier Guard. That is why two guardsmen stand watch outside the pub to this day.

The Publican

A Publican is a Diplomat, a Democrat, an Autocrat, an Acrobat and Doormat. He must have the facility to entertain Prime Ministers, Princes of Industry, Pickpockets, Gamblers, Bookmakers, Pirates, Philanthropists, Popsies and Prudes. He must be on both sides of the "Political Fence" and be able to jump that fence.

He should be, or have been, a Footballer, Golfer, Bowler, Tennis Player, Cricketer, Dart Player, a Sailor, Pigeon Fancier, Motor Racer and a Linguist, as well as have a good knowledge of any other sport involving Dice, Cards, Horse Racing and Billiards. This is most useful, as he has to settle arguments and squabbles. He must be a qualified Boxer, Wrestler, Weight Lifter, Sprinter and Peacemaker.

He must always look immaculate when drinking with the Ladies and Gentlemen mentioned above, as well as bankers, Swankers, Theatricals, Commercial Travellers and Company Representatives, even though he has just made peace between two, four, six or more of the aforementioned Patrons.

To be successful, Warren must keep the Pub full, the House full, the Storeroom full, the Wine-cellar full, the Customers full, and not get full himself. He must have staff who are Clean, Honest, Quick workers, Quick thinkers, non-Drinkers, Mathematicians and Technicians, and at all times be on the Boss' side, the Customers' side, and stay on the Outside of the Bar.

To sum up: Warren must be Outside, Inside, Offside, Glorified, Sanctified, Crucified, Stupefied, Cross-eyed, and if he's not the Strong Silent Type, there's always Suicide!!!
